Health & Wellness

Complete Guide to Pet Health & Wellness

Comprehensive information to ensure your pet lives a long, healthy, and happy life!

üè• Preventive Health Care

üíâ Vaccinations

Keep your pet protected with proper vaccinations. Core vaccines prevent serious diseases like rabies, distemper, and parvovirus.

Puppies: Start at 6-8 weeks, boosters every 3-4 weeks until 16 weeks
Kittens: Start at 6-8 weeks, boosters at 12 and 16 weeks
Adults: Annual or triennial boosters as recommended

ü¶ü Parasite Prevention

Protect your pet from fleas, ticks, heartworms, and intestinal parasites with year-round prevention.

Heartworm: Monthly preventive medication
Fleas & Ticks: Monthly topical or oral treatments
Deworming: Every 3-6 months or as recommended

ü¶∑ Dental Health

Dental disease affects 80% of pets by age 3. Regular dental care prevents pain, tooth loss, and systemic infections.

Daily: Brush teeth with pet-safe toothpaste
Weekly: Offer dental chews or toys
Annually: Professional dental cleaning

‚öïÔ∏è Common Health Issues

‚öñÔ∏è Obesity

Signs: Ribs not easily felt, no visible waist, difficulty moving

Prevention: Portion control, regular exercise, limit treats, avoid table scraps

Impact: Can lead to diabetes, arthritis, heart disease, reduced lifespan

ü§ß Allergies

Signs: Itching, skin redness, ear infections, excessive licking, hair loss

Types: Food allergies, environmental allergens, flea allergy dermatitis

Treatment: Consult vet for diagnosis, may need diet changes or medication

ü¶¥ Arthritis

Signs: Limping, difficulty standing, reluctance to jump or climb, stiffness

Management: Weight control, joint supplements, pain medication, gentle exercise

Prevention: Maintain healthy weight, provide soft bedding, avoid over-exercise

üëÇ Ear Infections

Signs: Head shaking, scratching ears, odor, discharge, redness

Causes: Bacteria, yeast, allergies, ear mites, moisture buildup

Prevention: Regular ear cleaning, keep ears dry, address allergies promptly

üíß Urinary Problems

Signs: Frequent urination, straining, blood in urine, accidents indoors

Common Issues: UTIs, bladder stones, kidney disease

Prevention: Fresh water always available, regular bathroom breaks, proper diet

ü§¢ Digestive Problems

Signs: Vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, loss of appetite, lethargy

Causes: Dietary indiscretion, food allergies, parasites, infections

Prevention: Consistent diet, avoid table scraps, slow food transitions

üçñ Nutrition & Weight Management

Essential Nutrition Guidelines

ü•© Protein Requirements

Dogs need 18-25% protein in their diet, while cats require 26-30% or more. Choose high-quality animal-based proteins as the primary ingredient.

üåæ Carbohydrates & Fiber

Provide digestible carbohydrates and fiber for energy and digestive health. Whole grains, vegetables, and fruits offer essential nutrients.

üíß Hydration

Fresh water should always be available. Pets need approximately 1 ounce of water per pound of body weight daily.

Life Stage Nutrition

üêæ Puppies & Kittens: High-calorie, nutrient-dense food to support growth. Feed 3-4 times daily until 6 months old.

üêï Adults: Balanced maintenance diet. Feed 1-2 times daily based on activity level and breed size.

üë¥ Seniors: Lower calorie, easily digestible food with joint support. May need specialized diets for health conditions.

ü§∞ Pregnant/Nursing: Increased calories and nutrients. May need puppy/kitten formula for proper nutrition.

üß† Mental Health & Behavioral Wellness

Signs of Good Mental Health

  • Alert and responsive to surroundings
  • Playful and engaged with toys and family
  • Healthy appetite and normal sleep patterns
  • Appropriate social interactions
  • Comfortable being alone for reasonable periods
  • Adapts well to routine changes

Addressing Behavioral Issues

Common issues like anxiety, aggression, or destructive behavior often stem from underlying causes. Consult with a veterinarian or certified animal behaviorist for proper assessment and treatment.

Separation Anxiety

Gradual desensitization, crate training, interactive toys, possible medication

Aggression

Professional evaluation, behavior modification, environmental management

Excessive Vocalization

Address underlying needs, training, mental stimulation, rule out medical issues

üë¥ Senior Pet Care

Pets age faster than humans. Dogs and cats are considered seniors around 7-10 years, depending on size and breed.

üè• Increased Vet Visits

Schedule wellness exams every 6 months. Early detection of age-related conditions improves treatment outcomes.

üõèÔ∏è Comfort Adjustments

Provide orthopedic beds, ramps or stairs, non-slip flooring, and easily accessible food and water bowls.

üíä Managing Conditions

Monitor for arthritis, dental disease, kidney issues, cognitive decline, and vision or hearing loss.
