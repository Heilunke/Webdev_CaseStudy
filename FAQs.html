Why it is important to adopt?

The importance of adopting a pet is that it gives an animal a second chance at life. Hundreds or thousands of pets end up in shelters every year, and choosing to adopt helps reduce the pets that are in need of care and the warmth of a loving family. At the same time, it eases the workload of our rescuers by freeing up resources and space for new animals in need. Here at Petlink, our pets are vaccinated, well cared for, and ready to become loving companions. Adopting a pet is not just about bringing a new family member; you are also saving a life and making a positive difference.

How can I adopt from PetLink?

Applicants go through a screening process to ensure that our rescued animals go to loving homes. The process includes an online interview via Google Meet and at least two separate shelter visits to meet and get to know your chosen pet.

Why is there an adoption fee?

The adoption fee is a token of your commitment and a demonstration of your financial capacity to care for a pet. P500 for cats / P1000 for dogs already covers your pet’s spay/neuter surgery, vaccinations, and tick+flea treatment.

I live in the province/abroad. Can I still adopt?

Yes, but special arrangements need to be made for the meet-and-greet depending on your location. Please contact us to discuss your options. You may also opt to adopt from your local pound instead.

Can I return my adopted pet if I change my mind?

A pet is a lifetime commitment. However, if you truly can’t keep your adopted pet, please don’t abandon them. Please return them to us so we can find another home for them.

Can my adoption application get denied?

Yes. Some reasons for denied applications include not being able to keep the pet indoors, incompatibility with the household, plus other circumstances that may be damaging to the health, safety, and happiness of our fur animals.

Do you have purebred cats or dogs?

It is very rare that purebred cats or dogs are admitted to the shelter. Please consider adopting a local breed instead.

What should I do if I see an injured stray animal?

You are their best chance of survival. Calling PetLink does not guarantee that the animal will be rescued due to limited space at the shelter and volunteers. However, our vet can assist in emergency cases if you are willing to transport and foster the animal.

How do I donate to PetLink?

We accept cash donations via bank transfer, Gcash, and Paymaya. Donations in kind are also accepted and can be delivered to the shelter or ordered from our partner shops. Check out our donation channels here.
